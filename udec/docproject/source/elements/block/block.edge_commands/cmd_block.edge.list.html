block edge apply list command

Syntax

block edge apply list <keyword> <range>

Primary keywords:

boundary-elements    displacement    fluid    fluid-nonwetting    forces    history    interior    state    thermal

Display boundary condition information. The foollowing keywords maye be used to display the information separately.

boundary-elements

boundary element conditions. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) associated block corner index
(3) associated gridpoint index
(4) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(5) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(6) associated boundary element node
(7) second boundary element node
(8) boundary element load-distribution factor
displacement

Displacments at boundary. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) \(x\)-displacement of boundary corner
(5) \(y\)-displacement of boundary corner
fluid

wetting fluid pressure and permeability condition. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) permeability conditions at boundary corner:
  0 permeable
  1 impermeable
(5) pore pressure at boundary corner
fluid-nonwetting

non-wetting fluid pressure and permeability condition. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) permeability conditions at boundary corner:
  0 permeable
  1 impermeable
(5) pore pressure at boundary corner
forces

Forces at boundary. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) total \(x\)-component of force at boundary
(5) total \(y\)-component of force at boundary
(6) \(x\)-component of force added for transient loading
(7) \(x\)-history applied type[1]
(8) \(y\)-component of force added for transient loading
(9) \(y\)-history applied type[1]

Note

The total forces also include the force induced by displacement boundary conditions.

history

histories[1] used to apply transient loads. The column headings are:

(1) history index
(2) history type[1]
(3) current value of history
interior

interior boundary loads. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) total \(x\)-component of force at boundary
(5) total \(y\)-component of force at boundary
(6) \(x\)-component of force added for transient loading
(7) \(x\)-history type[1]
(8) \(y\)-component of force added for transient loading
(9) \(y\)-history type[1]

interior boundary displacements. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) total \(x\)-component of displacment at boundary
(5) total \(y\)-component of displacement at boundary

interior boundary state. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) associated block corner index
(3) associated gridpoint index
(4) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(5) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(6) boundary condition in the \(x\)-direction
(7) boundary condition in the \(y\)-direction
(8) free-field boundary condition
(9) boundary condition in the shear direction
(10) boundary condition in the normal direction

interior boundary temperature. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) temperature at boundary corner

interior boundary wetting fluid pressure and permeability condition. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) permeability conditions at boundary corner:
  0 permeable
  1 impermeable
(5) pore pressure at boundary corner

interior boundary histories[1] used to apply transient loads. The column headings are:

(1) history index
(2) history type[1]
(3) current value of history

interior boundary velocities.

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) \(x\)-velocity of boundary corner
(5) \(x\)-history type[1]
(6) \(y\)-velocity of boundary corner
(7) \(y\)-history type[1]
state

boundary condition state. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) associated block corner index
(3) associated block index
(4) associated gridpoint index
(5) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(6) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(7) boundary condition in the \(x\)-direction
(8) boundary condition in the \(y\)-direction
(9) free-field boundary condition
(10) boundary condition in the shear direction
(11) boundary condition in the normal direction

Note

The condition numbers for \(x\)-, \(y\)-, normal and shear directions are:

0 free

1 stress (or force)

2 boundary element

3 viscous

4 velocity

The free-field indicator numbers are:

0 free field turned off

1 or 2 free field turned on

thermal

temperature at boundary. The column headings are:

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) temperature at boundary corner
vel

velocities at boundary corners.

(1) boundary corner index
(2) \(x\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(3) \(y\)-coordinate of boundary corner
(4) \(x\)-velocity of boundary corner
(5) \(x\)-history type[1]
(6) \(y\)-velocity of boundary corner
(7) \(y\)-history type[1]

..[1] History types are: sine function, cosine function, table and FISH function.
