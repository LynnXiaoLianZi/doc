anisotropic model properties

anisotropic
angle

orientation of plane of anisotropy taken counterclockwise from positive x-axis, \(θ\)

density

mass density, \(ρ\)

young-plane

elastic Young’s modulus in the plane of isotropy, \(E_1\)

young-normal

elastic Young’s modulus in the plane perpendicular to the plane of isotropy, \(E_2\)

shear-normal

elastic cross-shear modulus between plane of isotropy and perpendicular plane (i.e., \(x_1x_2\)- or \(x_2x_3\)-plane), \(G_{12}\)*

poisson-plane

Poisson’s ratio for the normal strain in the \(x_1\)-direction (in the plane of isotropy) related to the normal strain in the \(x_2\)-direction (in the perpendicular plane) due to uniaxial stress in the \(x_2\)-direction, \(ν_{21}\)

poisson-normal

Poisson’s ratio for the normal strain in the \(x_1\)-direction (in the plane of isotropy) related to the normal strain in the \(x_3\)-direction due to uniaxial stress in the \(x_3\)-direction, \(ν_{31}\)

* The cross-shear modulus, \(G_{12}\), for anisotropic elasticity must be determined. S. G. Lekhnittskii (Theory of Elasticity of an Anisotropic Body. Moscow: Mir Publishers, 1981) suggests the following equation based on laboratory testing of rock.

\[G_{12} = {{E_1E_2} \over {E_1(1+2ν_{12})+E_2}}\]

assuming the \(x_1x_3\)-plane is the plane of isotropy.
