block contact join command

Syntax

block contact join b keyword <range>

Primary keywords:

by-block    by-contact

The join command is used to prevent contstruction cuts that are made for geometric purposes from influencing the model results. In zoned models where there are matching gridpoints between the blocks, the gridpoints are slaved together. For rigid blocks or places where there are no matching gridpoints a special contact model with high stiffness and infinite strength is used. For rigid block models that do not have any unjoined contacts the stiffness properties must be specified. The stiffness properties may also be modified for zoned blocks models.

The command can be used to join or unjoin contacts. Joined contacts will be displayed with a constitutive model of 10. Contacts that are joined cannot slide or open. Contacts are joined for on; they are unjoined for off.

This command works best if used after all joints have been formed and the model has been zoned. Otherwise, the joining of the block edges may not be continuous.

The stiffness properties of the special constitutive joined contact model are determined automatically for zoned blocks or for models with unjoined contacts. The normal stiffness is based on an average zone stiffness or the largest joint stiffness assigned in the model, (whichever is greater) multiplied by a factor (BLOCK CONTACT JOIN-RATIO). The default factor is 100. The shear stiffness is half the normal stiffness. The commands to modify the joined contact stiffness properties are block contact join-stiffness-normal, block contact join-stiffness-shear, and block contact join-ratio.

There are two keywords that are used for the command. The choice of the keyword depends on the intent of the range that is specified.

by-block

Joins or unjoins all contacts between blocks with centroids in the (optionally) specified range. The range is used to select the blocks.

by-contact

Joins or unjoins all contacts in the (optionally) specified range. The range is used to select the contacts.
